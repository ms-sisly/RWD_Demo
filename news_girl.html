My Square-Toe Boots Are Killing Me

What Ones Should I Wear Now?, Dec 7, 2017

I’ve been wearing square-toe boots for about three years now, every fall and winter, specifically ones by the Ukrainian label Bevza. The designer, Svitlana Bevza, molded them off of a ’90s shape with a 90-degree angle—delicious. At the time, square-toe boots were becoming a phenomenon on the runways of Balenciaga and on the Instagram of the It-girl label By Far. A square toe is the pinnacle of bad-meets-good taste, and in the right context it becomes sleek. (No dull edges here.) But I’m all out of options, as my pair are falling apart and Bevza won’t make them for a while. In my grieving process, I’ve been exploring other types and have fallen in love with severe, razor-sharp pointy-toe shoes.

In one article, I praise those delicious dagger heels, and name-check the reality television patron saints of pointed pumps. Though that footwear is designated for 9-to-5 business and maybe a date night, pointed-toe boots are another story. They are crafted for the wear and tear of standard boots, but still have a catlike prowess of a sleek and sharp pump. This isn’t the same for round- or blunt-toe boots, which feel more appropriate for daytime dawdles, maybe horseback riding. A sharp toe comes with a longstanding mission.

For this story, I was browsing the archives of Vogue looking for images of pointed-toe boots, and behold, I found a page from September 1997, edited by Candy Pratts Price, that featured five mean-looking, calf-skimming boots from Manolo Blahnik, Marc Jacobs, and more. The writer Sarah Van Sicklen described them as: “After sensible shoes—think round, think clunky—it’s high time designers upped the danger quotient with pointed-toe, ice pick heels, and a heady dose of attitude.”

Zoom Fatigue?

Let These Statement-Collar Tops Liven Up Your WFH Look, Oct 1, 2021

Earlier this year, after seeing removable collars come down the fall runways at Erdem, JW Anderson, and Ganni, my colleague, senior fashion news writer Brooke Bobb, predicted that 2020 would be a banner year for the accessory. One thing we couldn’t have predicted however, is how much time we would really be spending on Zoom this year capturing our lifeless outfits on our webcams and broadcasting them across our colleagues’, friends’, and family’s screens. Luckily for us, the bold accessory trend has surely taken off, and added some vigor to our wardrobes.

Wearing a blouse or sweater affixed with a dramatic, lace or ruffled collar brings your look to the next level, and might even earn a delightful compliment or two. For those who want to make less of an obvious statement, there are white blouses with Victorian-inspired floppy crochet or lace collars, as well as those that pop in contrast atop dainty black sweaters and cardigans. In fact, one of the layered collars crafted from New York–based brand Sea was inspired by the late Ruth Bader Ginsburg, whose defining fashion statement was her lace collars.

For those who don’t want an overly formal option, don’t fret. You can still don one of these Zoom-worthy tops while staying comfortable, thanks to H&M, which has brought the lace collar to a crewneck sweatshirt, and Ganni, which has fastened theirs to a cozy, oversized cardigan. After a summer of using brightly colored, printed tops to liven up your on-screen wardrobe, let these statement-collar shirts, blouses, and sweaters be your work-from-home wardrobe heroes for fall.
